# Assignment 2: Industrial Lecture Summary — Global ERP Migration & SAP S/4HANA Evolution 🏢🎨

## 1. Project Summary
As part of my academic coursework in **Enterprise Systems Design and Modeling (SECJ3744)**, I compiled an analytical newsletter summarizing an exclusive industrial lecture featuring Patrick Thong, the Head of PPG's SAP Center of Excellence (COE) Operation Process for the Asia-Pacific region. The objective of this assignment was to evaluate how real-world multinational corporations manage large-scale digital transformations and analyze the operational logic behind transitioning core global business units into modern consolidated systems.

My summary highlights PPG's enterprise strategies and modular architectures across several domains:
* **Global ERP Infrastructure Migration:** Analyzes the multi-year "Connect PPG" modernization project, tracking the migration from legacy on-premises **SAP ECC 6.0** environments to the modern **SAP S/4HANA** platform. This massive overhaul unifies decentralized global operations onto a single, trusted source of data across Europe and Asia.
* **Integrated Process Module Breakdown:** Documents the real-world utility of specific functional software modules, demonstrating how manufacturing lines use **SAP MM** (Materials Management) and **SAP PP** (Production Planning) to control resource pipelines, while front-end workflows use **SAP SD** (Sales & Distribution) to manage cross-border customer deliveries.
* **Industrial Corporate Hiring Metrics:** Outlines modern technology workforce expectations, clarifying that top-tier companies look for professional ethics, curiosity, and learning adaptability rather than expensive specialized certifications during initial internship evaluations.

## 2. Reflection

**What I have learnt:**

* This industrial talk showed me the massive scope of managing corporate technical migrations. I learned that moving a global giant like PPG from SAP ECC 6.0 to S/4HANA is a multi-year strategic journey that requires careful planning across continents to successfully merge scattered datasets into one reliable source.
* Hearing directly from an Asia-Pacific operations leader deepened my understanding of module interdependencies. I realized that a seamless international supply chain is completely dependent on real-time data handshakes where sales activities instantly trigger factory production line schedules and warehouse purchasing loops.
* This lecture changed how I look at my personal professional growth. Learning that major enterprise employers prioritize a curious mind, enthusiastic problem-solving, and a strong work ethic over pre-existing technical platform training encourages me to focus on building a strong understanding of core design principles.
